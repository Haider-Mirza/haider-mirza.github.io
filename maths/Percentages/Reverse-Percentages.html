Reverse Percentages

When a percentage change has already happened and we want to know the original quantity, this is called a "reverse percentage". These are almost always answered wrongly

Examples

Example 1

A store has a 20% off sale. An item is marked as £8.20. How much is it before the sale.

Solution

Let's call the original price before the sale \(x\). \(x\) × 0.8 = 8.20 (Here we have a simple algebraic equation) \(x\) = 8.20 ÷ 0.8 \(x\) = £10.25

Notice that for reverse percentage questions, you divide by the multiplier.

Example 2

My house price has Appreciated by 3.8% since I bought it. It is now worth £200,000. How much was it worth before?

Note

Appreciation basically means increase. So the house price has been increaseed by 3.8%

Solution

Let's call the original price before the appriciation \(x\).

\(x\) × 1.038 = 200,000 \(x\) = 200,000 ÷ 1.038 (divide by the multiplier) \(x\) = £192,678.23
