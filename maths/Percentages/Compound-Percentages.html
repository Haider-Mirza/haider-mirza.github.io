Compound Percentages

Or, in other words, compound interest.

Examples

Example 1

Suppose I have £500 in the bank. My bank pays 2% interest a year. How much will I have after:

i) 2 Years ii) 10 Years iii) 100 Years

Solution

500 × 1.02 (1 year) 500 × 1.02 × 1.02 (2 years)

500 × 1.02 × 1.02 \(=\) 500 × 1.022 \(=\) £520.20

And you do the same with the rest of the questions (ii & iii)

Note

If we get the same amount of interest each year (2% of 500 = £10), this is called simple interest. We would only have £600! Almost nobody does this though but there is a change that this may come up in tests.

Example 2

Bacteria in a petri dish increase in number by 50% every 3 hours. We had 50 bacteria in a petri dish at midnight on Monday. How many will there be a week later?
